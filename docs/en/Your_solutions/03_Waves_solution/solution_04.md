## 4. Phase Difference

The phase difference between two points on a wave describes how far "out of sync" they are based on their spatial separation (path difference).

### Formula
The phase difference ($\Delta\phi$) in radians is calculated using the ratio of the distance between the points ($\Delta x$) to the total wavelength ($\lambda$):

$$\Delta\phi = \frac{2\pi \cdot \Delta x}{\lambda}$$

---

### Given Data
* **Path Difference ($\Delta x$):** $\frac{\lambda}{3}$
* **Full Cycle:** $2\pi$ radians

### Step-by-Step Calculation

1.  **Substitute** the given distance into the phase difference formula:
    $$\Delta\phi = \frac{2\pi \cdot (\frac{\lambda}{3})}{\lambda}$$

2.  **Simplify** the expression by canceling out the wavelength ($\lambda$) from the numerator and denominator:
    $$\Delta\phi = \frac{2\pi}{3}$$

3.  **Convert to Degrees** (Optional for context):
    Since $\pi$ radians $= 180^\circ$:
    $$\frac{2 \cdot 180^\circ}{3} = 120^\circ$$

---

### Final Result
The phase difference between two points separated by $\lambda/3$ is **$2\pi/3$ radians**.

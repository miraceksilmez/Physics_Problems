# Energy Stored by Charge in a Capacitor

## Given

- Plate area:
  \[
  S = 0.02 \, m^2
  \]

- Distance between plates:
  \[
  d = 5 \, mm = 0.005 \, m
  \]

- Voltage:
  \[
  U = 500 \, V
  \]

- Vacuum permittivity:
  \[
  \varepsilon_0 = 8.85 \times 10^{-12} \, F/m
  \]

---

# 1. Capacitance \(C\)

Formula for a parallel-plate capacitor:

\[
C = \varepsilon_0 \frac{S}{d}
\]

Substitute the values:

\[
C = 8.85 \times 10^{-12} \times \frac{0.02}{0.005}
\]

\[
C = 3.54 \times 10^{-11} \, F
\]

\[
\boxed{C = 35.4 \, pF}
\]

---

# 2. Energy Stored in the Capacitor

Energy formula:

\[
W = \frac{1}{2} C U^2
\]

Substitute the values:

\[
W = \frac{1}{2} \times (3.54 \times 10^{-11}) \times (500)^2
\]

\[
W = 4.43 \times 10^{-6} \, J
\]

\[
\boxed{W = 4.43 \, \mu J}
\]

---

# 3. Electric Field Intensity \(E\)

Formula:

\[
E = \frac{U}{d}
\]

Substitute the values:

\[
E = \frac{500}{0.005}
\]

\[
E = 1.0 \times 10^5 \, V/m
\]

\[
\boxed{E = 100000 \, V/m}
\]

---

# 4. Force of Attraction Between Plates

Formula:

\[
F = \frac{1}{2} \varepsilon_0 E^2 S
\]

Substitute the values:

\[
F = \frac{1}{2} \times (8.85 \times 10^{-12}) \times (1.0 \times 10^5)^2 \times 0.02
\]

\[
F = 8.85 \times 10^{-4} \, N
\]

\[
\boxed{F = 0.000885 \, N}
\]

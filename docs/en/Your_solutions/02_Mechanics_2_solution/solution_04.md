# Physics Solution: Energy and Momentum (Inelastic Collision)

This problem involves a two-stage physical process: a block sliding down a frictionless track (Energy) and a perfectly inelastic collision (Momentum).

---

## 1. Problem Statement

* **Block 1 ($m_1$):** 0.5 kg, starts from a height of **3.0 m**.
* **Block 2 ($m_2$):** 1.5 kg, initially at rest at the bottom.
* **Action:** Block 1 slides down and sticks to Block 2.
* **Goal:** Find the final speed ($v_f$) of the combined mass.

---

## 2. Stage 1: Sliding Down (Conservation of Energy)

As Block 1 slides down the frictionless track, its gravitational potential energy is converted into kinetic energy.

$$m_1gh = \frac{1}{2}m_1v_1^2$$

### Solving for $v_1$:
$$v_1 = \sqrt{2gh}$$

Substituting the values:
$$v_1 = \sqrt{2 \cdot 9.8 \cdot 3.0}$$
$$v_1 = \sqrt{58.8}$$
$$v_1 \approx 7.668 \, \text{m/s}$$

*This is the speed of Block 1 just before it hits Block 2.*

---

## 3. Stage 2: Collision (Conservation of Momentum)

Since the blocks stick together, this is a **perfectly inelastic collision**. Total momentum is conserved, but kinetic energy is not.

$$\sum p_{initial} = \sum p_{final}$$
$$m_1v_1 + m_2v_2 = (m_1 + m_2)v_f$$

### Substituting the values:
*(Note: $v_2 = 0$ because it is at rest)*

$$0.5 \cdot 7.668 + 1.5 \cdot 0 = (0.5 + 1.5) \cdot v_f$$
$$3.834 = 2.0 \cdot v_f$$

### Solving for $v_f$:
$$v_f = \frac{3.834}{2.0}$$
$$v_f = 1.917 \, \text{m/s}$$

---

## 4. Final Results Summary

| Parameter | Symbol | Value |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Initial Height | $h$ | $3.0 \, \text{m}$ |
| Speed before collision | $v_1$ | $\approx 7.67 \, \text{m/s}$ |
| Combined Mass | $M$ | $2.0 \, \text{kg}$ |
| **Final Speed** | $v_f$ | **$\approx 1.92 \, \text{m/s}$** |

---

## 5. Conclusion
The combined blocks move at a final speed of **$1.92 \, \text{m/s}$**. A significant amount of kinetic energy was lost during the collision, having been converted into heat and sound during the "sticking" process.

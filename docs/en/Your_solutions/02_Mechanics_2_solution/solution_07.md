# Problem Statement (precise)

A 5 kg block is placed on top of a 10 kg block.  
A horizontal force \( F = 45 \, \text{N} \) is applied to the 10 kg block.  
The 5 kg block is tied to a wall, so it remains stationary.  
The coefficient of kinetic friction between all moving surfaces is \( \mu = 0.2 \).  

Find the acceleration of the 10 kg block.

---

# Step 1: Identify interactions and motion

- The **10 kg block moves right**.
- The **5 kg block is fixed** → relative motion exists between blocks.
- Friction appears in two places:
  1. Between bottom block and ground
  2. Between top and bottom block

---

# Step 2: Friction with the ground

Normal force from ground:
\[
N_{\text{ground}} = (m_1 + m_2)g = (5 + 10)\cdot 9.8 = 147 \, \text{N}
\]

Friction:
\[
f_{\text{ground}} = \mu N = 0.2 \cdot 147 = 29.4 \, \text{N}
\]

Direction: left

---

# Step 3: Friction between blocks

Normal force:
\[
N_{\text{top}} = m_1 g = 5 \cdot 9.8 = 49 \, \text{N}
\]

Friction:
\[
f_{\text{top}} = \mu N = 0.2 \cdot 49 = 9.8 \, \text{N}
\]

Direction: left (opposes motion of bottom block)

---

# Step 4: Total opposing force

\[
f_{\text{total}} = f_{\text{ground}} + f_{\text{top}} = 29.4 + 9.8 = 39.2 \, \text{N}
\]

---

# Step 5: Net force on the 10 kg block

\[
F_{\text{net}} = 45 - 39.2 = 5.8 \, \text{N}
\]

---

# Step 6: Apply Newton’s Second Law

\[
a = \frac{F_{\text{net}}}{m_2} = \frac{5.8}{10} = 0.58 \, \text{m/s}^2
\]

---

# Final Answer

\[
a = 0.58 \, \text{m/s}^2
\]

Direction: **to the right**

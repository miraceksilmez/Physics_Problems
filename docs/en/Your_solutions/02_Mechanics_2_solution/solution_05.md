# Physics Solution: Inelastic Collision Analysis

This document analyzes the momentum and energy dynamics of a runner jumping onto a stationary cart.

---

## 1. Problem Statement

* **Runner ($m_1$):** 70 kg, moving at **3 m/s**.
* **Cart ($m_2$):** 140 kg, initially **at rest** ($v_2 = 0$).
* **Action:** The runner jumps onto the cart, and they move together.

**Objectives:**
1.  Find the **final speed ($v_f$)** of the cart and runner.
2.  Determine if **kinetic energy is conserved** and explain why.

---

## 2. Part A: Calculation of Final Speed

In any collision where no external horizontal forces act, **Linear Momentum is conserved**. Since the runner and cart move together after the collision, this is a **perfectly inelastic collision**.

### Momentum Conservation Formula:
$$P_{initial} = P_{final}$$
$$m_1v_1 + m_2v_2 = (m_1 + m_2)v_f$$

### Step-by-Step Substitution:
1.  **Initial Momentum:**
    $$P_i = (70 \cdot 3) + (140 \cdot 0) = 210 \, \text{kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$$
2.  **Total Mass:**
    $$M_{total} = 70 + 140 = 210 \, \text{kg}$$
3.  **Solving for $v_f$:**
    $$210 = 210 \cdot v_f$$
    $$v_f = 1 \, \text{m/s}$$

**Result:** The final speed of the cart with the runner is **$1 \, \text{m/s}$**.

---

## 3. Part B: Kinetic Energy Conservation Analysis

To check for energy conservation, we compare the total kinetic energy before and after the collision.

### 1. Initial Kinetic Energy ($KE_i$):
$$KE_i = \frac{1}{2}m_1v_1^2 + \frac{1}{2}m_2v_2^2$$
$$KE_i = \frac{1}{2}(70)(3)^2 + 0$$
$$KE_i = 35 \cdot 9 = 315 \, \text{Joules}$$

### 2. Final Kinetic Energy ($KE_f$):
$$KE_f = \frac{1}{2}(m_1 + m_2)v_f^2$$
$$KE_f = \frac{1}{2}(210)(1)^2$$
$$KE_f = 105 \, \text{Joules}$$

### Comparison:
* **$\Delta KE = KE_f - KE_i = 105 - 315 = -210 \, \text{J}$**

---

## 4. Explanation and Conclusion

**Is kinetic energy conserved?** > **No**, kinetic energy is **not conserved**. 

### Why?
In this collision, approximately **66.7% of the initial kinetic energy was lost**. 
In a **perfectly inelastic collision** (where objects stick together), kinetic energy is always lost. This energy is not "destroyed" but rather transformed into other forms, such as:
* **Internal Energy (Heat):** Generated by friction between the runner's shoes and the cart.
* **Deformation/Vibration:** The mechanical work required for the runner to stabilize on the cart.
* **Sound Energy:** The noise produced during the impact.

| **Total Kinetic Energy** | $315 \, \text{J}$ | $105 \, \text{J}$ |
| **Velocity** | $3 \, \text{m/s}$ (Runner) | $1 \, \text{m/s}$ (Combined) |

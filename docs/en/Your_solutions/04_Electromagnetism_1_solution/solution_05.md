# Field Levitation – Solution

## Problem
What electric field strength is required to make a proton levitate against Earth's gravity at the surface of the Earth?

Given:

- Proton mass: `m = 1.67 × 10⁻²⁷ kg`
- Proton charge: `q = 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ C`
- Gravitational acceleration: `g = 9.8 m/s²`

---

## Concept

To make the proton levitate, the electric force must balance the gravitational force.

There are two forces acting on the proton:

### 1. Gravitational Force (downward)

F_g = mg

This force pulls the proton downward because of Earth's gravity.

### 2. Electric Force (upward)

F_e = qE

This force pushes the proton upward because the proton has a positive charge.

---

## Equilibrium Condition

For levitation, the net force must be zero.

That means:

qE = mg

Solving for the electric field:

E = mg / q

---

## Calculation

Substitute the values:

E = (1.67 × 10⁻²⁷ × 9.8) / (1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹)

E = (1.6366 × 10⁻²⁶) / (1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹)

E = 1.02 × 10⁻⁷ N/C

---

## Final Answer

E ≈ 1.0 × 10⁻⁷ N/C

Direction: **Upward**

The electric field must point upward so that the electric force balances gravity and keeps the proton suspended.

---

## Conclusion

The proton levitates when the upward electric force equals the downward gravitational force. By setting these two forces equal, we find the minimum electric field required for equilibrium.

# Field Levitation – Presentation Solution

## Problem

What electric field strength is required to make a proton levitate against Earth's gravity at the Earth's surface?

### Given:

- Proton mass: `m = 1.67 × 10⁻²⁷ kg`
- Proton charge: `q = 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ C`
- Gravitational acceleration: `g = 9.8 m/s²`

---

## Step 1: Analyze the forces

A proton is affected by two forces:

### Gravitational force (downward)

F_g = mg

This force pulls the proton downward.

### Electric force (upward)

F_e = qE

This force pushes the proton upward because the proton is positively charged.

---

## Step 2: Apply equilibrium condition

For levitation, the proton must remain at rest.

So the net force must be zero:

qE = mg

Solve for E:

E = mg / q

---

## Step 3: Substitute the values

E = (1.67 × 10⁻²⁷ × 9.8) / (1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹)

E = (1.6366 × 10⁻²⁶) / (1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹)

E = 1.02 × 10⁻⁷ N/C

---

## Final Answer

**E ≈ 1.0 × 10⁻⁷ N/C**

**Direction: Upward**

The electric field must point upward so that the electric force balances the gravitational force.

---

## Presentation Summary

To make the proton levitate, the upward electric force must equal the downward gravitational force.  
By equating these two forces and solving for the electric field, we find the required field strength.
